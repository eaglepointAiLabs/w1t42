Construct a TrailForge Sports Training & Community Portal supporting athletes, subscribers, coaches, and operations staff in a single offline-ready web experience. Regular Users log in with a username and password to maintain a personalized home feed in a Vue.js interface that blends training activity, course updates, and locally ingested sports news; each feed item provides “Not Interested,” “Block Author,” and “Block Tag,” with immediate UI feedback and a confirmation toast, and the feed de-duplicates similar items seen in the last 7 days while offering cold-start recommendations based on selected sports and first-week browsing. Users can create and edit running/cycling/walking records with duration, distance (miles), calories, heart rate, pace, tags, notes, and an optional location entered as free text or chosen from previously saved places (no maps), and can attach a GPX file imported from their device for later viewing as a simple coordinate list. After a completed course or service order, users can post reviews with a 1–5 rating, text, and up to 5 images (PNG/JPEG, max 5 MB each), add one follow-up review within 30 days, and view threaded replies from Coaches or Support Agents. Review dimensions (e.g., punctuality, coaching quality, facility cleanliness) are configurable by Administrators, with user-controlled anonymous vs real-name display; disputes are handled via an appeal button visible for 7 days after posting, leading to an arbitration timeline and status messages that close the loop with service-quality improvements.

The backend uses Koa to expose decoupled REST-style APIs consumed by the Vue.js frontend, with MySQL as the system of record for users, follow graph, activities, orders, reviews, appeals, risk tags, content sources, and analytics snapshots. The platform runs fully offline on a single on-prem host; authentication is local only, passwords are hashed with bcrypt, sensitive profile fields are encrypted at rest, and role-based access controls separate Admin, Coach, Support Agent, and Regular User permissions. Reviews and content governance are enforced server-side using a locally maintained sensitive-word dictionary, image format validation plus hash-based deny lists, anti-fake-review controls (one review per order, max 2 reviews per day per user, and risk flags when multiple accounts share a device fingerprint), and display rules that hide content under arbitration. Service-quality management tracks dimension scores, complaint rates, and resolution times, automatically tagging high-risk users after 3 upheld violations and supporting blacklists that restrict reviewing for 30 days. Payment and fund security are implemented as an offline ledger that records “WeChat Pay” transactions via batch-imported reconciliation files from a cashier workstation, verifies locally stored signatures on imported payment results, supports refunds and partial refunds down to $0.01, auto-cancels unpaid orders after 30 minutes, and guarantees consistency through idempotency keys plus retry/compensation jobs backed by a MySQL queue table. Sports news ingestion is handled by scheduled jobs that process locally mirrored RSS/API payload files and HTML crawl-rule extracts dropped into monitored folders, with allow/block lists, rate limits to protect ingestion endpoints, three automatic retries on failure, and immutable ingestion logs for auditing. Operations and analytics provide an offline back office with enrollment funnel, course popularity, renewal and refund rates, channel performance, instructor utilization, and location revenue/cost reporting, including custom filters and CSV export with access logging.
